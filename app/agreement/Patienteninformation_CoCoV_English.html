This data privacy statement informs you about the purpose, type, and scope of the data collected by the CoCov app. The application comprises the CoCov app (hereinafter referred to as "app") and the backend server of the Institute of Medical Systems Biology at Ulm University. Here, the analysis takes place. This means that the data transmitted is not only processed by the app, but also by the backend server.

We take the protection of your personal data very seriously and work in full compliance with the applicable data protection regulations. The following paragraphs will inform you about this:

(1) the purpose for which the data are collected

(2) what is the legal basis for the processing,

(3) how long we keep the data and where they are stored,

(4) which personal data are collected via the CoCov app,

(5) why age, sex, vaccine, and vaccination date are recorded and stored

(6) that no data is passed on to third parties,

(7) what rights you have as a user and how you can exercise them.

1. Purpose of data collection and processing

Proving data about occurred adverse events after vaccination with one of the new corona-vaccines helps the University Hospital of Ulm to gain an overview of possible symptoms. Moreover, this offers the possibility to detect previously unknown and rarely occurring symptoms. Findings from the study are to be made available to the public in an anonymous form. For this purpose, your data will be evaluated together with the data of all other app users (hereinafter "purpose").

It is emphasized explicitly that the app does not provide medical advice or individual diagnostics.

2. Consent to data processing

The processing of data is carried out based on your consent to the processing of special categories of personal data (health data) in addition to the purpose mentioned above (art. 6 para. 1 lit. a and art. 9 para. 2 lit. a DSGVO).

3. Scope of the data collected, storage location, and duration

3.1 We process the personal data of the users mentioned in section 4 for the intended purpose and following the statutory provisions. For the above-mentioned purpose, your age, sex, vaccine, and time of vaccination are recorded. The data transfer of collected symptoms takes place via a pseudonym automatically generated by the app. Please note, that a data transfer is only possible if you agreed previously to the data transfer.

3.2 Which data are stored for the purpose is stated in section 4. At any time, the University Hospital Ulm stores directly identifying information such as name or address. Only the data mentioned in section 4 is stored.

3.3 The automatically generated pseudonym displayed in the app enables you to exercise your data subject rights. Please handle it carefully and keep it protected from access by third parties. If you lose your pseudonym, you will no longer have the opportunity to request the deletion of your data.

3.4 By agreeing to take part in the study and entering symptoms after vaccination with one of the new corona vaccines, you are sending your data and permitting the data mentioned under 4.2 to be stored and processed for the above-mentioned purpose.

3.5 The retrieval of the data logged by you takes place between your smartphone and the server of the Institute of Medical Systems Biology at Ulm University. This data is transmitted in encrypted form to the server operated by us exclusively in Germany, where it is processed, and the data mentioned in section 4.2 is stored.

3.6 Your data will be deleted as soon as the purpose of the data collection and processing mentioned in section 1 ceases to apply, At the latest, your data will be deleted after 10 years. Until then, your data will be stored in a pseudonymised form on a server of the Institute of Medical Systems Biology at Ulm University. In accordance with Guideline 17 of the "Guidelines for Safeguarding Good Scientific Practice" of the German Research Foundation (as of September 2019), data will be stored for up to 10 years. Only the evaluation results will be published for research purposes in an aggregated and anonymised form. This does not allow any conclusions to be drawn about your person. Moreover, this aggregated and anonymised data may be stored permanently in a scientific research database.

3.7 If you no longer wish to use the app, you can delete it. If you also wish to delete the data you have already transferred to us, you can do this before deleting the app under the menu item "Delete my data". Data that has already been included and published in evaluations at this point cannot be removed retroactively, as it has only been included in the evaluations in anonymised form. However, data that has not yet been evaluated in anonymised form will not be used for future evaluations and will be deleted. Due to technical circumstances, the complete deletion of data can take up to 30 days. The stated deletion period of 30 days refers to data storage in backups. In the active system, the data is deleted within a maximum of 24 hours, usually much faster.

4. Retrieval and storage of personal data

4.1 The individual use of the app is based on a pseudonym.

4.2 The following personal health data is transmitted to the server of the Institute of Medical Systems Biology at Ulm University and stored there:

General data:

- Age

- Gender

- Vaccine

- Number of doses

Local reactions and systemic events recorded by you:

- Pain at the injection site

- Redness at the injection site

- Swelling at the injection site

- Fatigue

- Headache

- Chills

- Fever (>38°C)

- Muscle pain

- Joint Pain

- Nausea

- Diarrhea

and how these symptoms have gradually changed.

5. Storage of age, sex and vaccination date

To use the app, it is necessary to enter age (rounded to 5 years), gender, and the number of received doses, as these are included in the evaluation in order to obtain a more accurate picture of vulnerable groups.

6. Transfer of data to third parties

6.1 Personal data is treated by us as strictly confidential and is not passed on to third parties.

6.2 The CoCov study does not transmit any data to analysis services such as Google Analytics or social platforms such as Facebook.

7. Data security

7.1 We restrict access to information submitted to us to those employees who need access in order to provide the service. These employees are contractually bound to comply with the statutory data protection provisions.

7.2 The app was developed by the Institute of Medical Systems Biology at Ulm University, Albert-Einstein-Allee 11, 89081 Ulm. It operates the app on behalf of and under the instructions of the University Hospital of Ulm (order processing).

7.3 To protect your data, extensive technical and organisational measures have been implemented (e.g. firewalls, encryption and authentication techniques, procedural instructions).
